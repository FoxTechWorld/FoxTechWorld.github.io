Rant: You'll never be productive

January 1, 1

Table of Contents

    • Introduction
    • Sell the idea of productivity
    • Conclusion

Introduction

I’m sorry to say this, but you’re an idiot if you watch productivity videos, and I don’t mean to be sarcastic or cruel to you.

Productivity videos sell the idea that complex things increase your productivity, and the truth is the opposite.

Simple things increase productivity, a note increases productivity if you’re smart, because if you write down a task and forget the note, you’ll forget the task.

True productivity begins with three recursive principles:

  1. writing
  2. Save content
  3. Reading

If you remember to write, but read and save before writing, it can be more productive than opening a complex application, writing and forgetting.

Sell the idea of productivity

Many productive videos bombard viewers with the message that adopting complex tools or methods is the key to unlocking productivity nirvana.

They showcase flashy software, intricate time management techniques, and elaborate productive hacks as the holy grail of efficiency.

However, this approach is often misleading and can lead individuals down a rabbit hole of inefficiency rather than productivity, the more classic example is “life hacks” a small diy to make things more efficiently (or not).

Examples of Complex Methods and Tools

One common example touled in productivity videos is use of sophisticated task management applications. These apps promise to revolutionize your workflow by offering a pherora of features, from intricate project organization to detailed progress tracking. And ever is bullshit, in the end these tools may seem appealing on the surface, they often come with a steep learning curve and require significant time investment to mastery.

Another endorsement of complex time management methodologies, such as the Pomodoro Technique or the Eisenhower Matrix.

While these frameworks can be useful in certain contexts, their rigid structures may not stuff everyone working style.

Moreover, adhering to strict time management rules can become counterproductive if they impede spontaneity and creativity.

The Misleading Nature of Complexity

The allure of complexity in productivity is akin to the allure of a magic pill: it promises quick and effortless results.

However, just like with miracles cures, the really often falls short of the hype.

Complex Tools and methods may initially seem impressive, but they can quickly become burdensome and overwhelming.

Further to some, the pursuit of complexity often distracts individuals from focusing on what truly matters: taking the action.

Instead of getting bogged down in the minutiae of mastering intricate systems, productivity is best achieve through simplicity and practically.

In programming, we have a concept: If it is much complex, we adopt the posture of Divide and Conquer

Emphasizing Simplicity and Practicality

True productive lies in simplicity: Instead of chasing after the latest productivity fads, individuals should prioritize simple, actionable steps that align their goals and values.

This could mean jotting down tasks on a piece of paper, setting aside dedicated time for focused work, or taking regular breaks to recharge.

By embracing simplicity, individuals can eliminate unnecessary complexity from their workflow and streamline their productive efforts.

Rather than being elaborate tools and methods, they can focus on getting things done efficiently and effectively.

Conclusion

Productive videos that peedle the idea of complexity as secret to productive are doing a disservice to their viewers.

Instead of falling for the allure of complexity, individuals should embrace simplicity and practicality in their pursuit of productivity.

By focusing on straightforward, actionable strategies, they can achieve real and sustainable productivity gains.
